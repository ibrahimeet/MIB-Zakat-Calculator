What is Zakat?

Zakat is one of the Five Pillars of Islam — an obligatory annual charity of 2.5% on wealth that exceeds the Nisab threshold and has been held for one complete lunar year (Hawl).

📐 How to Calculate
1
Add all your zakatable assets (cash, gold, silver, investments, business)
2
Subtract short-term debts due within this lunar year
3
Check if your net wealth meets the Nisab threshold
4
If yes, pay 2.5% of your net zakatable wealth
✅ What to Include
✅ Zakatable (Include)
Cash at home · Bank savings · Gold & silver · Stocks & MFs · Business inventory · Receivables · Rental income savings · Cryptocurrency
❌ Not Zakatable (Exclude)
Primary home · Personal car · Household furniture · Personal clothing · Business machinery · Long-term locked pension
